Incredible Kerala

Pleaces to Visit in Kerala

Kerala, often referred to as “God’s Own Country”, is a beautiful state located along the Malabar Coast in the southwestern part of India. Known for its breathtaking natural beauty, Kerala is a land of lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, picturesque hill stations, and sun-kissed beaches. The state is crisscrossed by an extensive network of rivers and canals, making backwater tourism—especially houseboat rides in places like Alleppey and Kumarakom—a major attraction. Kerala’s hill stations such as Munnar and Wayanad offer cool climates, tea and spice plantations, and scenic views, drawing nature lovers and tourists from around the world. The state also boasts a rich cultural heritage, with classical art forms like Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, and Theyyam, and is known for its vibrant festivals like Onam and Vishu, which reflect the deep-rooted traditions of its people.

Kerala is also a pioneer in health and education, with the highest literacy rate in India and a strong emphasis on public health and social development. Its ancient Ayurvedic practices attract wellness seekers globally. The cuisine of Kerala is another highlight, offering a blend of spicy flavors and coconut-rich dishes such as appam, puttu, and Kerala-style fish curry. The state's diverse religious landscape includes beautiful temples, mosques, and churches, reflecting its history of religious harmony and tolerance. With its unique mix of natural beauty, cultural richness, and progressive society, Kerala stands out as one of India’s most sought-after travel and living destinations.

Munnar

Munnar is a stunning hill station nestled in the Western Ghats mountain range in the Idukki district of Kerala, India. Situated at an altitude of around 1,600 meters (5,200 feet) above sea level, Munnar is renowned for its sprawling tea plantations, misty mountains, and cool, pleasant climate throughout the year. Once the summer retreat of the British colonial rulers, Munnar today is a top tourist destination that attracts nature lovers, honeymooners, and adventure seekers alike. The town is surrounded by lush green hills covered with neatly manicured tea gardens, which create a breathtaking landscape and offer opportunities for plantation walks and tea factory visits.

Munnar is also rich in biodiversity and is home to the Eravikulam National Park, where the endangered Nilgiri Tahr can be spotted. During certain years, the hills come alive with a rare phenomenon—the blooming of the Neelakurinji flower, which paints the valleys in shades of blue once every 12 years. Popular attractions around Munnar include Mattupetty Dam, Kundala Lake, Top Station, and Attukal Waterfalls, each offering scenic views and peaceful experiences. The region also supports a variety of wildlife and bird species, making it a haven for photographers and eco-tourists. With its serene atmosphere, charming landscape, and refreshing weather, Munnar offers a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of city life.

Backwaters

The backwaters of Kerala are a unique and enchanting network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons that run parallel to the Arabian Sea coast. Spanning several districts, including Alappuzha (Alleppey), Kumarakom, Kollam, and Kochi, these tranquil waterways are among the most iconic features of Kerala’s landscape. Formed by the action of waves and shore currents creating low barrier islands, the backwaters are fed by 38 rivers and stretch over 900 kilometers. One of the best ways to experience the backwaters is by taking a houseboat cruise—locally known as kettuvallam—which offers a slow, peaceful journey through coconut palm-fringed shores, paddy fields, and traditional village life.

The backwaters are not just a tourist attraction but also play a vital role in the lives of local communities, serving as transportation routes and sources of livelihood through fishing and agriculture. Kumarakom and Vembanad Lake, the largest lake in Kerala, are particularly famous for bird watching and eco-tourism, while the Alappuzha backwaters are known for hosting the Nehru Trophy Boat Race, a vibrant and competitive snake boat race held annually. The serene beauty, gentle lapping of water, and scenic views make the backwaters a perfect retreat for relaxation, honeymoon, or cultural exploration. A visit to Kerala is considered incomplete without experiencing the magic of its backwaters.
